**Subject:** Private request: check of a nonclumpiness application in parallel chip-firing

Dear [Professor/Dr./First name and surname],

I am seeking a narrow technical check of an application of the
Jiang-Scully-Zhang nonclumpiness theorem to a candidate proof of the general
middle-stair conjecture posed by Ji, Li, and Wang.

The proposed new ingredient is an activity-density lemma, not the firing-word
argument: a returning orbit with activity below one half has at most
`2|E|-|V|` chips, and the affine complement supplies the high-activity bound.
In the strict middle band this forces activity one half. I then use Theorem 6.2
of *Motors and Impossible Firing Patterns in the Parallel Chip-Firing Game* to
conclude that each balanced cyclic firing word alternates, yielding a two-step
state return.

Would you be willing to check whether this cyclic formulation and use of
Theorem 6.2 are faithful, including when the chosen return length is not
assumed least? The bundle includes a conventional proof and a Lean 4
formalization of the full argument, including a kernel-checked version of the
finite mischief certificate. The formal check is evidence of correctness, not
novelty or a substitute for comparison with the published argument.

This is a private pre-publication request. I have made no priority claim and
have not posted the draft publicly.
